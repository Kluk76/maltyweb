## 🔴 THREE SKU IDENTITY LAYERS — a packaging EVENT can carry three DIFFERENT SKUs (operator-clarified 2026-05-28, ingest mis-routing bug pending)
Until 2026-05-28 the model treated `ref_skus` as a SINGLE namespace per `bd_packaging_v2` event. **It is NOT.** A single packaging event has up to THREE distinct SKU-identity layers and they can ALL differ on the same row:
1. **Brewing recipe** (`bd_packaging_v2.recipe_id_fk` → `ref_recipes.id`) — what LIQUID was brewed. Brewing-side aliases re-point `recipe_id_fk` via the ingest's recipe-resolution map (e.g. BLO→ZEP `ref_recipes.id=57`, BLA→MOO `id=44`). Pure liquid identity.
2. **Packaging SKU** (`bd_packaging_v2.sku_id_fk` → `ref_skus.id`) — what PACKAGE the liquid went into. Production-side output identity. **CAN differ from the recipe-implied SKU** when the recipe is a white-label/relabel: e.g. a ZEP-recipe brew packaged into BLO-branded 4-pack cans → recipe ZEP + SKU BLO4C (id 6), NOT ZEP4C (id 56). The packaging-SKU is the canonical handle for everything downstream (COGS, BOM, production dashboards, tank/vendable HL).
3. **Sales SKU** (the code on the customer-facing eshop/B2B sales record) — what is SOLD. **CAN differ from the packaging SKU due to UNBOXING** at the eshop layer: eshop opens a ZEPC 24-pack box and re-sells the cans as ZEP4C 4-packs or ZEPCU singles. Lives on the sales feed (`inv_sales_order_lines` / `inv_sales_bc`), NOT on `bd_packaging_v2`.

**Live BLO/BLA white-label examples** (the operator-confirmed semantics):
- **BLO** ("Blonde des Romands") — a relabel brand. Recipe 10 (Blonde des Romands as its OWN recipe) was brewed 2021-01 → 2022-03 (16 brews only); from **2022-04 onwards BLO is a ZEP-recipe relabel** (brewing alias `BLO → ref_recipes.id=57`). BLO packaging SKUs: BLO4=5, BLO4C=6, BLOF=7.
- **BLA** ("Blanche des Romands") — ALWAYS a relabel (no own recipe). Brewing alias `BLA → MOO ref_recipes.id=44`. BLA packaging SKU: BLA4=4 (inactive). The first live white-label in the BOM model (mig 148; see §BLA4 WHITE-LABEL).
